Methods

MultiPAC

MultiPAC (Seitz, 2020) is a mult-parameter approach to assess riverbed clogging, which consists of measuring multiple riverbed physico-chemical parameters, namely riverbed:

  • grain size characteristics
  • porosity
  • intersticial dissolved oxygen (IDO)
  • hydraulic conductivity (kf)
The grain size and riverbed porosity are derived from freeze cores and represent bulk parameter values for a given xy measurement point. The interstitial dissolved oxygen (IDO) and hydraulic conductivity (kf) are measured as vertical profiles along the riverbed depth and thus enable to locate depths layers with low hyporheic habitat quality.

FlowTracker2

The FlowTracker2 is an ADV measurement device which enables to determine flow velocity vectors in all directions (x, y, and z). Through these, we can calculate turbulent kinetic energy in 3d (kt) an 2d (tke 2d, using only the components in x and y) to investigate morphological features for habitat enhancement. In addition, the velocity and water depth data allow the validation of hydrodynamic numerical models.

Surface and subsurface sediment sampling

Sieving analyses of surface and subsurface samples from the overlayer and underlayer, respectively, enable toderive several sedimentological parameters that characterize the substrate.
